### DAY 6: The Introvert's Summer Survival Guide

**Subject Line:** For the introverts who are tired of being "on"

**Email Body:**

Summer is loud. Summer is social. Summer expects you to be *available*.

Here's how to survive without becoming a hermit:

**THE INTROVERT'S SUMMER RULES:**

1. **One social thing per weekend, max.**
   - Saturday OR Sunday, not both
   - Protect your recovery time

2. **The "appearance fee" strategy.**
   - Show up for 45 minutes
   - Leave while you're still having fun
   - "I have another commitment" (your couch counts)

3. **The outdoor loophole.**
   - Sit in a park alone with a book
   - Technically "out," actually recharging

4. **The rain check collection.**
   - Collect invites you never intend to use
   - "That sounds great, let's plan something soon!"
   - Soon = never, and that's okay

5. **The honest boundary.**
   - "I love you, but I need a quiet weekend"
   - Real friends understand

### DAY 18: Saying No to FOMO (Without FOMO About Saying No)

**Subject Line:** The FOMO about FOMO

**Email Body:**

You said no to the plans.

Now you're worried you're missing out on missing out.

**HOW TO STOP THE SECOND-GUESSING:**

**When the doubt creeps in:**

1. **Name it:** "I'm having FOMO about my choice"
2. **Remember why:** "I said no because I needed rest"
3. **Trust your past self:** "She knew what she needed"
4. **Do something present:** "Right now, I'm [reading/walking/napping]"
5. **Let it pass:** "This feeling will pass in 20 minutes"

**THE TRUTH:**
The thing you said no to? It's probably fine. You're probably not missing anything life-changing.

Your rest is more important than their party.
